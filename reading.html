Reading Passage

Global festivals are a **vibrant** showcase of human culture, each offering a unique lens into societal values. The Rio Carnival in Brazil, renowned for its **exuberant** samba parades and **lavish** costumes, draws millions annually, blending music, dance, and community spirit. In contrast, Diwali, the Hindu festival of lights celebrated across India, involves lighting **myriad** oil lamps to signify the triumph of good over evil, accompanied by fireworks and shared feasts. Germanyâ€™s Oktoberfest, a **quintessential** Bavarian tradition, features hearty meals, folk music, and vast quantities of beer, fostering a sense of regional pride. However, the **encroachment** of globalization has introduced commercial elements to these events, sometimes diluting their cultural **authenticity**. For instance, corporate sponsorships at festivals like Carnival can overshadow traditional practices, raising debates about whether modernization erodes their original meaning or enhances their global appeal. These celebrations, while diverse, share a common thread of uniting people, yet their evolution prompts reflection on preserving heritage in a rapidly changing world.

Comprehension Questions

  1. What is the main purpose of the Rio Carnival?
  2. How does Diwali symbolize its core theme?
  3. What aspect of Oktoberfest reflects Bavarian culture?
  4. What concern is raised about global festivals?

Sample Answers: 1. Samba parades and celebration. 2. Victory of good over evil with lamps and fireworks. 3. Beer and folk music. 4. Loss of authenticity due to commercialization.

Key Vocabulary

  • Vibrant: Full of energy and enthusiasm.
  • Exuberant: Joyfully unrestrained and enthusiastic.
  • Lavish: Extravagantly rich or elaborate.
  • Myriad: A countless or extremely large number.
  • Quintessential: Representing the perfect example of something.
  • Encroachment: Gradual intrusion or takeover.
  • Authenticity: The quality of being genuine or real.
